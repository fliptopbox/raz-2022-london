    About this training:

    In this workshop we will analyze how the hip-joints serve as the “launching pad” for efficient walking and upright carriage and hone our observational skills. We want to recognize in the student their self-organization and gait patterns. The ability to observe those is essential for Functional Integration teachers to access the potential for long-lasting change. We will enrich our understanding by examining the functional anatomy of the hip joints, by analyzing videos of walking, running and athletic performances. Each day will be accompanied by an Awareness Through Movement lesson that is specifically chosen to highlight the functional relationships that will be explored in Functional Integration. The functionality of our hip joints is a marvel! On the one hand, they enable freedom in all three dimensions of movement. And on the other hand, they “carry” the task of supporting the body in non-stable conditions. These supposedly opposing properties, mobility and stability, are in fact synergistic and serve upright organization gracefully.

    Afterwards you will be able to:

  • Recognise how the hip joints work in unison to create efficient support and mobility.
  • Apply some functional anatomy of the hip-pelvic girdle relating to walking.
  • Identify some functional insights from an evolutionary perspective.
  • Observe and engage with your students’ gait pattern.
  • Examine how the hip joints serve as a link between the distal and the proximal.
  • Integrate an FI lesson and help the student “carry” the lesson better.
