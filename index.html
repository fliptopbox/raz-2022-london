About this training:

The functionality of our hip joints is a marvel!

On the one hand, they enable freedom in all three dimensions of movement. And on the other hand, they “carry” the task of supporting the body in non-stable conditions.

These supposedly opposing properties, mobility and stability, are in fact synergistic and serve upright organization gracefully.

In this workshop we will analyze how the hip-joints serve as the “launching pad” for efficient walking and upright carriage.

We will also train our observational skills needed to recognize the student’s self-organization and gait patterns. The ability to observe those is essential as Functional Integration teachers for assessing our student’s potential for long-lasting change.

We will enrich our understanding by examining the functional anatomy of the hip joints, by analyzing videos of walking, running and athletic performances.

Each day will be accompanied by an Awareness Through Movement lesson that is specifically chosen to highlight the functional relationships that will be explored in Functional Integration.

Afterwards you will be able to:

  • Recognise how the hip joints work in unison to create efficient support and mobility.
  • Apply some functional anatomy of the hip-pelvic girdle relating to walking.
  • Identify some functional insights from an evolutionary perspective.
  • Observe and engage with your students’ gait pattern.
  • Examine how the hip joints serve as a link between the distal and the proximal.
  • Integrate an FI lesson and help the student “carry” the lesson better.

Raz Ori Feldenkrais Trainer

Raz Ori is a Practitioner and Trainer of the Feldenkrais Method®. He has served on the educational staff of training programs in Tel-Aviv, New-York, Germany, Italy, and South Korea, teaching with Ruty Bar, David Zemach-Bersin, and Lior Pessach.

He graduated in 2001 from the Tel-Aviv Feldenkrais Professional Training Program, and trained for two more years with Yochanan Rywerant, one of Dr. Feldenkrais’ first students.

Raz runs The Ramat-Aviv Feldenkrais Center where he teaches Awareness Through Movement classes and gives individual Functional Integration lessons.

Raz gained clinical experience working for 15 years at Clalit Integrative Medicine, part of Israel’s largest public health provider. He has also worked at the Sheba Medical Center with disabled war veterans suffering from chronic pain, phantom limb pain, PTSD, and head injuries.

Raz is also a certified JKA practitioner working with special needs children. He teaches advanced trainings and conducts on-line mentoring groups.

Raz lives in Tel-Aviv, with his wife and children.
